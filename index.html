Half of All Brooklyn Based Evictions Carried Out In Just Six Neighborhoods

By: Pete Tomao, Dec. 18, 2021

New York City Marshals have carried out 18,906 evictions in Brooklyn since 2017 (Dataset linked here). Six Brooklyn community districts account for more than half of all evictions. Of Brooklyn's 18 community districts, the East New York neighborhood had the most evictions and the third highest proportion of severely rent burdened residents(2444 evictions and 31% of residents paying more than 50% of their income in rent).

According to the US census, median monthly rent in the borough has risen 20% over the past 10 years and now stands at $1426 per month (Dataset linked here). Many renters are just a pay check away from missing rent, 28% of Brooklynites spend more than half their income on rent.

“We’ve obviously lost a lot of affordable housing in the community over the last several decades...it's at crisis level the need,” Michelle de la Uz, Executive Director, Fifth Avenue Committee.

The Fifth Avenue Committee is a major provider of affordable housing in Brooklyn. De la Uz notes a recent housing lottery for 86 affordable units received 67,000 applications.
